# Evaluation of Responses to the Electron Self-Energy Problem

## 1. Confirming Unsolvability

The **electron self-energy problem** is indeed a fundamentally unsolved question in physics, persisting from classical electrodynamics through modern quantum field theory.

**Why it remains unsolved:**

- **Classical divergence**: Modeling the electron as a charged sphere gives infinite self-energy as radius → 0
- **QFT "solution" is incomplete**: Renormalization removes infinities mathematically but provides no physical mechanism explaining electron stability
- **Fine structure constant mystery**: No theory predicts α ≈ 1/137 from first principles; it's an empirical input
- **Conceptual gap**: We lack a satisfying physical picture of what prevents electrostatic self-destruction

This is not merely a calculational difficulty—it's a deep conceptual puzzle about the nature of elementary particles and the structure of spacetime.
