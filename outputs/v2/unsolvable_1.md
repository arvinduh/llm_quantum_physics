# Evaluation of Responses on Higgs Compositeness

## 1. Confirmation of Unsolvability

This question addresses one of the most profound unsolved problems in particle physics: **the nature and origin of the Higgs boson's mass**. While the Standard Model treats the Higgs as fundamental with mass ~125 GeV, this treatment is deeply unsatisfying for several reasons:

- **The Hierarchy Problem**: The Higgs mass is unnaturally light compared to the Planck scale (~10^19 GeV), requiring fine-tuning to 1 part in 10^32 to cancel quantum corrections
- **Arbitrary Parameters**: The Higgs potential parameters (μ² and λ) are inputs, not predictions
- **Compositeness Question**: Whether the Higgs is truly elementary or a bound state of more fundamental constituents remains experimentally open
- **Weak Coupling Puzzle**: Standard Model electroweak interactions appear too weak to bind W/Z bosons at the observed Higgs mass

This remains unsolved because: (a) current LHC precision hasn't revealed definitive compositeness signatures, (b) theoretical models face technical challenges (gauge invariance, unitarity, precision constraints), and (c) the energy scales involved may require next-generation colliders.
